# Gurugram Air Pollution and Traffic Growth Analysis

## Overview
This project investigates the relationship between urban air pollution, traffic growth, and environmental health in Gurugram, Haryana, India. The analysis focuses on identifying key pollutants, seasonal patterns, meteorological influences, and the role of rapid growth in private vehicle ownership. The study also explores policy implications and sustainable urban planning strategies to mitigate pollution.

The insights generated in this project were used to prepare a **research paper manuscript** based on the results obtained from data analysis.

---

## Research Paper
The repository also contains the **research paper manuscript formatted in research paper (RP) style**, which presents the full study including introduction, methodology, results, discussion, and conclusions derived from the analysis performed in this project.

---

## Objectives
The main objectives of this study are:

- Analyze air pollution trends in Gurugram using publicly available datasets.
- Identify dominant pollutants and seasonal variations.
- Examine the influence of meteorological parameters on pollution levels.
- Study the relationship between increasing vehicle ownership and pollution.
- Evaluate policy measures and urban planning strategies for sustainable environmental management.

---

## Data Sources
This project uses **secondary datasets obtained from publicly available sources**, including:

- CPCB Air Quality and Meteorological Data
- Kaggle Air Pollution Dataset (compiled from official sources)
- Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) – Road Transport Year Book
- World Health Organization (WHO) reports
- World Bank environmental and transport reports

**Note:**  
Only processed data and analysis outputs are included in this repository. Original reports and external datasets are **not uploaded here**. Links to the official sources are provided instead.

## Key Analysis Performed

The analysis includes:

- Average pollutant concentration analysis
- Seasonal variation of pollution levels
- Meteorological parameter influence on air quality
- Monthly pollution trends
- Identification of highest and lowest pollution days
- Relationship between wind speed and pollutant behavior
- Growth trends in vehicle ownership
- Road infrastructure expansion vs vehicle density

---

## Key Insights

Some of the important findings include:

- PM₁₀ is the dominant pollutant observed in Gurugram.
- Pollution levels peak during winter and decrease during monsoon months.
- Rapid growth in private vehicle ownership contributes significantly to traffic congestion and emissions.
- Infrastructure expansion has not kept pace with vehicle growth.
- Non-exhaust emissions such as tyre wear particles (TWPs) contribute significantly to particulate pollution.
- Long-term environmental improvements require integrated policy interventions and stronger public transport systems.
